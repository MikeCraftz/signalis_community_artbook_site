SIGNALIS COMMUNITY ARTBOOK

Non‐commercial fan project : Community-assembled art archive

About The Game

SIGNALIS is a survival horror game developed by Rose‐Engine. It combines classic survival horror gameplay with a unique aesthetic and narrative.


More information can be found on the official website The game was released on 27th October 2022. It is available on PC, Xbox One, PS4 and Nintendo Switch.. I strongly recommend playing it if you a fan of classic survival horror games like Resident Evil or Silent Hill, or fan of retro aesthetics and anime.



About This Project

This is a community‐driven artbook celebrating the game SIGNALIS. It is a non‐commercial fan project and is not affilated with Rose-Engine. This artbook serves as a platform for artists to showcase their work inspired by SIGNALIS.


Each piece reflects a personal connection: what the game meant to us, how we interpreted it, and how we chose to express that through art. It is more than just an album of art, but with artist interpretations of the game, it is a collection of memories and experiences shared by the community, as well as what brought the artist to create their art.


I hope everyone who contributed to this project, as well as those who read it, will find something that resonates with them.



About The Book

Why is this book special?

This handcrafted artbook is designed to look like and in-universe user manual for the game SIGNALIS. Main inspiration for the design is the in-game UI, as well as older game user manuals and artbooks like the Homeworld technical manual.


What is in this book?

The book feature pages that are designed to matches the aesthetic of the game. Each artist is to feature their SIGNALIS fanart in this book, together with a monochrome, bakemonogatari-style that describes who they are, with a short description of their art and what it means to them.


Where can I get this book?

Once the book is completed, it will be available for free as a PDF. The book will also be published online across various platforms in digital format for free, likely on my social media.


All collaborators will receive a free physical copy of the book.


A small number of physical copies will be available for sales as well if I am able to secure a booth space at C107 Winter Comiket 2025, Tokyo, Japan. Any one participated the book as collaborator will be given a physical copy of the book for free. All proceeds from the sales will be used to cover printing costs for the book, transportation, booth space and other related costs are covered by me personally. However per rose-engine's policies , no online sales of the physical print will be made available, and the print will be a one-off print run.

What is the art requirement?


  • Up to 5 pieces of art can be submitted, with a maximum of 4 pages per artist, including the artist introduction page.
  • No unaltered in-game assets, official textures, models, marketing materials, logos, or character sprites from SIGNALIS may be used.
  • Please avoid using AI art as submission.
  • Only original fan artwork created by the submitting artist may be accepted.
  • You will be credited and acknowledged as a collaborator/team member of the project.
  • All submitted works must be related to SIGNALIS in theme, character, or aesthetic.
  • Artworks must not depict explicit sexual content, hate speech, or any offensive, obscene, or disrespectful material.
  • I will do my best to include as much as possible, please understand that due to printing costs and page limits, not all submissions may be featured in the final book.


What is the format?


  • Size should not be too large, I prefer it to be able to fit an A4 paper.
  • 300 dpi.
  • .png format
  • Title of your art in format of "Title", "Month+Year of creation"
  • Short Description / Commentary. Talk about your inspiration, references, or the meaning behind the piece. Or anything about the art pieces!
  • I will do my best to include as much as possible, please understand that due to printing costs and page limits, not all submissions may be featured in the final book.

Where can I submit and how does selection work?



If you are interested in contributing, please submit your application to the application form.

  • I will ask to get some sample of your art, and review your application.
  • Selection is base on number of arts you submit, their sizes as well as generally speaking do they match the intention of the book.
  • If you are selected, I will give you another private submission form to submit your final art.
  • I will then make a quick sample of how will your art look like, you can request any changes to the sample.

Do note that I might not be able to accept all submissions, as I have a limited number of pages available in the book.
